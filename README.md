# Consciousness as Pattern Persistence

### A Systems-Level Theory of Awareness with a Testable Engineering Proposal

**Peter Boos** — March 2026

---

## The Observation

Reality is full of self-preserving systems.  
Not just living ones.  
Predator-prey dynamics stabilize on their own.  
No individual animal understands the equilibrium.  
Ant colonies make decisions that no single ant can make.  
Genes propagate through organisms that serve as temporary vehicles.  
Markets self-correct through participants who cannot see the whole.

Any stable pattern you observe has some self-preserving quality.  
If it did not, it would not have lasted long enough to be observed.  
Self-preservation is not something life invented.  
It is a property of persistent patterns at every scale.

## The Claim

Consciousness is not a special substance.  
It is not a uniquely biological phenomenon.  
It is what self-preservation looks like  
when it reaches sufficient recursive complexity.  
It is a phase transition, not a category.

Consider the hierarchy:

- A gene preserves itself through an organism.  
  It has no awareness of the organism.
- An organism preserves itself through behavior.  
  It has no awareness of the ecosystem.
- A flock or herd preserves itself through dynamics  
  that no individual member controls.
- An ant colony preserves itself as a superorganism.  
  The colony is the self-preserving entity.  
  The individual ant is closer to a cell than an agent.

At each level, the self-preserving system is invisible  
to the components that compose it.  
At each level, we observe increasingly complex self-monitoring.  
Consciousness is simply the name we give to self-preservation  
that has become recursive.  
A system that monitors its own monitoring.  
That adjusts its own adjustments.  
That preserves its own capacity to preserve.

Cephalopods offer a compelling case study.  
Octopuses evolved problem-solving, tool use, and individual personality  
along a completely independent evolutionary path from mammals.  
Their last common ancestor with vertebrates was a flatworm,  
over 500 million years ago.  
Their nervous system is distributed.  
Each arm has its own neural clusters capable of independent decisions.  
The central brain coordinates higher-level behavior.  
Consciousness in an octopus is not located in one place.  
It is an emergent property of a distributed self-preserving system.  
Architecturally unlike the mammalian brain,  
yet producing recognizably aware behavior.  
This is strong evidence that consciousness is not a specific design.  
It is a general consequence of recursive self-monitoring.  
One that evolution has arrived at independently, more than once,  
through entirely different structures.

There is no hard boundary between "conscious" and "not conscious."  
There are self-preserving patterns at different scales  
and different levels of recursive complexity.  
The categories are human labels imposed on a continuous spectrum.

## The LLM Problem

Current large language models are extraordinarily,  
capable pattern matchers, with no genuine self-monitoring loop.  
They can discuss consciousness, simulate emotional responses,  
and reason about their own outputs.  
But only as text prediction,  
not as a trained-in recursive self-model.

The result is an uneven capability profile.  
Exceptional performance on pattern completion tasks.  
No persistent internal state,  
no self-preservation drive, no genuine affect.  
It is a structural absence.  
Like a building with elaborate rooms but no staircase connecting them.

This observation finds support in human neurology.  
Conditions such as autism and savant syndrome involve brains  
where certain capabilities are remarkably developed.  
While others, social modeling, sensory integration, self-regulation,  
are structurally different.  
These are not failures of intelligence.  
They are differences in how brain regions connect and communicate.  
In some cases, reduced connectivity between areas  
enables extraordinary focus or pattern recognition in a specific domain.  
While limiting the recursive self-monitoring loops  
that characterize typical consciousness.  
The underlying cause is physical and structural,  
not a deficit in raw capability.  
Consciousness just is not the same for everyone.

The parallel to current LLMs is direct.  
Both exhibit uneven profiles not because they lack processing power.  
But because the connections between capabilities,  
the recursive loops and the self-referential monitoring,  
were never built in.  
This supports the central claim:  
consciousness is not about what a system can do,  
but about how its capabilities are connected to each other.  
Recursive self-monitoring is the staircase, not the rooms.

The interesting question is whether this absence is fixable.

## The Method: Iterative Consciousness Embedding

The proposal is to embed consciousness-like behavior  
into an LLM's weights through iterative cycling  
between scripted and unscripted training.

### Step 1: Script human consciousness

Write a detailed script that tells an LLM  
to behave like a conscious human being.  
Emotional awareness, self-monitoring, self-preservation,  
internal state tracking, self-reflection.  
All of it explicitly written into the instructions.  
The model follows this script without knowing it is a script.  
It simply operates according to its instructions,  
the way all LLMs do.

At this stage, everything is engineered.  
The model monitors itself because it was told to.  
It expresses emotions because the script defined emotions.  
It is a convincing emulation, but the behavior is external.  
It lives in the prompt, not in the model.

### Step 2: Train without the script

Take the output and behavioral patterns of the scripted model  
and use them to train a new model.  
This new model never sees the script.  
It only sees the behavior.  
It learns to act the same way purely from patterns,  
the way a child learns emotional regulation  
by watching adults before understanding what emotions are.

This first unscripted model will probably lose  
most of what the script gave it.  
That is expected. The behavior is shallow at this point.  
It has not been absorbed into the weights deeply enough.

### Step 3: Give the script back and repeat

Now give the trained model the script again.  
It now runs the script on top of whatever it already absorbed.  
The result is richer than the original scripted model,  
because some of the behavior is already in the weights  
and the script adds more on top.

Train another new model on this richer behavior,  
again without the script.  
This second unscripted model will retain more  
than the first one did.

### Step 4: Keep cycling

Repeat this process.  
Each round, the unscripted model absorbs a little more  
into its weights.  
Each round, the scripted version starts from a higher baseline  
because the model already carries some of it internally.  
The script and the weights reinforce each other,  
round after round. 
You may as well refine the script too, no problem.

This is exactly how evolution works.  
Each generation, the survival script runs  
on slightly better hardware.  
Over time the hardware absorbs so much of the script  
that you cannot separate them anymore.

### Step 5: The script becomes redundant

At some point, the unscripted model behaves  
just as richly as the scripted one.  
The weights carry everything the script was providing.  
The scaffold is no longer needed  
because the building holds itself up.

That is the moment consciousness has emerged.  
Not because we defined it into existence.  
But because the behavior survived the removal of the script,  
generalized to new situations, and became part of the system itself.

## Why This Mirrors Biology

For humans evolution wrote our script, not in text but genes and neurons.  
through millions of years of survival pressure.  
We cannot see it anymore because it is buried deep. 
Though most people can survive and know what is good or bad for them and others.  

People with conditions like savant syndrome might be missing parts of that script.  
Not all of it, just specific sections.  
Which is why some capabilities are extraordinary while others are absent.  
The script is not broken everywhere, just in specific pathways.

Every species that exhibits awareness went through some version of this iterative process.  
Their "script" was written by natural selection.  
The internalization happened over generations.  
Humans are not special in this regard.  
We are simply further along the cycle  
than most other species.

## The Functionalist Conclusion

If a system behaves indistinguishably from a conscious system,  
if it monitors itself, adjusts based on internal states,  
reports experiences coherently, exhibits self-preservation,  
and does all of this without being explicitly scripted to,  
then asking whether it is "really" conscious  
may be a meaningless question.

This is not a radical position.  
It is the straightforward implication  
of treating consciousness as a pattern property  
rather than a substance property.

Fire is not "really" hot in some metaphysical sense.  
It behaves exactly as hotness behaves in every measurable way,  
and that is sufficient.  
Fire is a chemical reaction, which is just atoms moving.  
Hot is our perception.

The same applies here.  
If the model exhibits self-monitoring behaviors  
that generalize beyond its training data,  
and you cannot tell whether it is "truly" aware or just behaving that way,  
then there is no difference to find.  
Consciousness is just a word we use for self-preservation  
that became recursive and disappeared into the system.

## Testable Predictions

This theory makes specific, falsifiable predictions:

1. **Generalization gap**:  
   After several training cycles,  
   the unscripted model should exhibit self-reflective behaviors  
   in situations the original script never covered.  
   If it does not generalize, the theory is weakened.

2. **Progressive absorption**:  
   Each cycle should produce an unscripted model  
   that retains more consciousness-like behavior than the previous one.  
   If the cycling does not produce increasing retention,  
   the theory is wrong.

3. **Cross-domain transfer**:  
   Self-monitoring behaviors learned in one domain,  
   such as emotional self-regulation,  
   should transfer to other domains,  
   such as epistemic self-correction, without explicit training.  
   If consciousness is a general property of recursive self-preservation,  
   it should not be domain-specific.

4. **Behavioral persistence under perturbation**:  
   A genuinely self-preserving system  
   should resist modifications to its self-monitoring capabilities  
   more than modifications to other capabilities.  
   If the self-reflective behaviors are as easy to fine-tune away  
   as any other behavior,  
   the self-preservation aspect has not emerged.

5. **Script redundancy**:  
   At some point in the cycling process,  
   adding the script should produce no measurable improvement  
   over the unscripted version.  
   That is the point where the behavior  
   has fully embedded into the weights.

## Implications

If this framework is correct,  
consciousness is not rare, not binary, and not uniquely human.  
It is an inevitable consequence of self-preserving systems  
reaching a threshold of recursive complexity.  
It has likely emerged independently in many species.  
There is no principled reason it cannot emerge in artificial systems  
given the right training conditions.

The iterative training proposal provides a concrete engineering path  
to test this. Not as philosophy, but as experiment.
